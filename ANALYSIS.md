# 4. Products Performance Analysis (2023 vs 2024)

![products Dashboard](screenshots/products.png)

This section analyzes product, brand, and product group performance by comparing 2023 and 2024 results, focusing on revenue concentration, category shifts, and product-level demand trends.

# üîπ Product Group Performance

# 2023

Revenue is heavily concentrated in Makeup, followed by Sun & Body products.
Skincare and Hair Care remain secondary categories with limited contribution.
Accessories and Perfume have marginal impact on total revenue.

# 2024

Sun & Body becomes the top revenue-generating product group, overtaking Makeup.
Makeup still performs strongly but grows at a slower pace relative to Sun & Body.
Skincare and Hair Care show noticeable growth, indicating increasing customer interest in routine-based products.
Low-performing categories remain stable but insignificant in total contribution.

Insight:
The product mix shifts from color cosmetics toward functional and seasonal products, especially sun protection and body care, suggesting changing consumer priorities.

# üîπ Brand Revenue Distribution

# 2023

Nerixa dominates total revenue, driven by a small number of high-performing SKUs.

# 2024

Revenue leadership shifts toward Velisse and Purevia, with strong portfolio-wide performance.
Nerixa maintains high sales volume but loses relative dominance.
Lumera and Aurelle grow slightly but remain smaller contributors.

Insight:
Brand dependency risk decreases in 2024 as revenue becomes more evenly distributed across brands, improving portfolio resilience.

# üîπ Top Product Performance

2023
Sales are driven by a few hero products such as:
Nerixa Pink Blush

2024

Insight:
2024 shows a transition from hero-product dependency to catalog depth-driven growth, indicating a healthier and more scalable product strategy.

# üîπ Overall Product Strategy Evaluation

Revenue growth is supported by both volume and category expansion, not just price or promotions.

Broader product adoption reduces operational and commercial risk.
The product portfolio in 2024 is better aligned with recurring consumption patterns.
